HVA

High-Value Asset

Description

In the context of incident response phases, HVA stands for High-Value Asset. This term refers to critical assets within an organization that are essential for business operations, financial integrity, and overall security posture. Identifying HVAs is crucial during incident response, as these assets often require prioritized protection and recovery efforts. HVAs can include sensitive data, proprietary software, financial systems, and critical infrastructure components. The incident response team must focus on these assets during the preparation, detection, analysis, containment, eradication, and recovery phases to minimize potential damage. A comprehensive understanding of HVAs allows organizations to allocate resources effectively, implement targeted security measures, and ensure swift recovery in the event of a cybersecurity incident. Consequently, HVAs play a vital role in risk management and can significantly influence the overall resilience of an organization against cyber threats.

Examples

  • Customer databases containing sensitive personal information
  • Financial transaction systems that manage monetary exchanges

Additional Information

  • Identifying HVAs is part of the asset management process in cybersecurity.
  • Incident response plans should include specific strategies for protecting HVAs.
