TTP

Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures.

Description

In the context of incident response phases, TTP stands for Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures. This term is used to describe the behavior and methodology of cyber adversaries when they execute attacks. Tactics refer to the high-level goals or objectives of an adversary, such as data exfiltration or system disruption. Techniques are the general methods used to achieve these goals, for example, phishing or exploiting vulnerabilities. Procedures are the specific implementations of these techniques, detailing how they are executed in real-world scenarios. Understanding TTPs is crucial for incident response teams as it helps them anticipate potential attacks, recognize indicators of compromise, and formulate effective countermeasures. By analyzing TTPs, organizations can enhance their security posture, improve threat detection capabilities, and respond more effectively to incidents. This knowledge is often collected from threat intelligence feeds and shared within the security community to stay ahead of evolving threats.

Examples

  • Phishing emails used to gain initial access (Technique) under the tactic of Credential Access.
  • Using a specific malware variant to exfiltrate data (Procedure) as part of the tactic of Data Theft.

Additional Information

  • TTPs are fundamental components of the MITRE ATT&CK framework.
  • Studying TTPs can help organizations build better detection rules and incident response plans.
